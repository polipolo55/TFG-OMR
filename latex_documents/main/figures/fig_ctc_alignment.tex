\caption[CTC alignment by example.]{CTC alignment by example.  The
encoder emits a per-frame argmax over $W/4$ feature frames
(\code{C}, \code{5}, \code{q}, \code{dot} abbreviate the LMX tokens
\code{pitch:C}, \code{octave:5}, \code{quarter}, \code{dot};
$\emptyset$ is the blank class).  The decoder collapses runs of
consecutive identical tokens---here \code{C\,C}~$\to$~\code{C} in
row~2---and then drops every blank, recovering the LMX sequence
\code{C\,5\,q\,dot\,dot} (a double-dotted C-octave-5 quarter note).
The highlighted blank between the two \code{dot} frames is the
\emph{separator}: without it, the two \code{dot} tokens would collapse
to one in row~2 and the double-dot would be lost.  This is the
canonical reason the blank symbol exists in CTC---it lets the model
emit the same token twice in a row.  Many different per-frame
alignments collapse to the same output, which is why CTC trains
without per-frame supervision and tolerates a compressed time axis
$W/4$ strictly wider than the output length $N$.}
