\caption[Encoder--decoder transformer architecture for OMR (SMT style).]{%
  The encoder--decoder transformer used in OMR systems such as
  SMT~\cite{riosVilaSheetMusicTransformer2024} and
  SMT++~\cite{riosVilaSMTPlusPlus2024}, drawn in two phases.
  The \emph{encoder} splits the sheet-music image into patches, adds
  positional encodings, and applies $N$ self-attention layers to produce an
  encoder memory of patch embeddings. The \emph{decoder} generates the
  output sequence one token at a time: at each step it embeds the
  previously emitted tokens, runs $N$ decoder layers (masked self-attention,
  cross-attention into the encoder memory, and a feed-forward block), and
  projects the result to the next token. The thick arrow is the
  cross-attention link; the dashed arrow is the autoregressive feedback that
  appends each predicted token to the decoder input at step $t{+}1$.
  Unlike CRNN--CTC, no explicit segmentation or conditional-independence
  assumption is imposed, and the target may be any linear encoding
  (\code{**kern}, ABC, or LMX).}
