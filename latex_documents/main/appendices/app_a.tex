\chapter{Additional Tables and Hyperparameters}
\label{app:hyperparams}

This appendix collects the exact hyperparameters, architectural sizes, augmentation probabilities, dataset filters and inference defaults used throughout the project. It is reference material: the body chapters cite the relevant table when an exact configuration is needed to reproduce a result. Unless otherwise stated, the values reported here are the defaults of the \texttt{Config} dataclass in \texttt{src/CRNN\_CTC/config.py}, which is serialised into every checkpoint at training time.

\section{Offline scan-simulation augmentation}

\Cref{tab:aug} lists the augmentation operations applied by \texttt{src/data\_processing/augment\_scanned.py} to produce the \texttt{scanned/} copies of every clean LilyJAZZ render. The albumentations block is applied first; the post-processing steps are applied afterwards in the order shown. An additional lightweight online jitter (brightness $\pm 5 \%$, contrast bias $\pm 3 \%$, Gaussian noise $\sigma \approx 0.01$, $\pm 2$ px horizontal shift) is applied during training only with probability $\texttt{online\_aug\_prob} = 0.5$.

\begin{table}[h]
\centering
\begin{tabular}{lll}
\toprule
\textbf{Transform} & \textbf{Parameters} & \textbf{Probability} \\
\midrule
\multicolumn{3}{l}{\textit{Albumentations block (geometric + photometric)}} \\
ElasticTransform            & $\alpha = 22$, $\sigma = 4.5$            & 0.82 \\
GridDistortion              & 5 steps, distort $\pm 0.08$              & 0.72 \\
OpticalDistortion           & distort limit 0.12                       & 0.38 \\
Affine                      & rot. $\pm 4^{\circ}$, shear $\pm 1.2^{\circ}$, scale 0.97--1.03, translate $\pm 1.5 \%$ & 0.85 \\
GaussianBlur                & $\sigma \in [0.2, 1.1]$                  & 0.62 \\
Sharpen                     & $\alpha \in [0.15, 0.45]$, lightness $\in [0.85, 1.0]$ & 0.48 \\
RandomToneCurve             & scale 0.12                               & 0.72 \\
GaussNoise                  & std range $[0.012, 0.045]$               & 0.78 \\
RandomBrightnessContrast    & brightness $\pm 0.06$, contrast $[0.03, 0.18]$ & 0.70 \\
ImageCompression            & JPEG quality $[72, 92]$                  & 0.35 \\
\midrule
\multicolumn{3}{l}{\textit{Post-processing steps (applied in order)}} \\
\texttt{dilate\_ink}        & 0, 1, or 2 morphological iterations (weights 0.42 / 0.48 / 0.10) & 1.0 \\
\texttt{vary\_staff\_line\_thickness} & $\pm 1$ px morphological tweak on detected horizontals  & 0.40 \\
\texttt{remap\_tones}       & linear remap to paper range $[28, 245]$  & 1.0 \\
\texttt{add\_vignette}      & radial darkening, strength $\approx 0.15$ jittered $\pm 40 \%$ & 0.50 (exclusive) \\
\texttt{add\_uneven\_illumination} & directional gradient, max strength 0.25, side $\in \{\text{left, right, top}\}$ & 0.35 (exclusive) \\
\texttt{add\_halftone\_lines} & period $\in [4, 9]$ rows, strength $\in [0.03, 0.07]$ & 0.25 \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\caption{Parameters of the offline scan-simulation augmentation pipeline. Vignette and uneven illumination are mutually exclusive: a single roll selects one, the other, or neither, with the marginal probabilities reported.}
\label{tab:aug}
\end{table}

\section{Training-time dataset filters}

The three filter flags in \Cref{tab:filters} realise the lead-sheet domain contract described in \Cref{ch:design}. They are applied at dataset construction time inside \texttt{OMRDataset}, not during data generation: the renderer and the converter write every sample to disk, and the filters decide which of those samples participate in training.

\begin{table}[h]
\centering
\begin{tabular}{lll}
\toprule
\textbf{Flag} & \textbf{Default} & \textbf{Effect} \\
\midrule
\texttt{filter\_multi\_staff}        & True & Drops samples whose original PNG height exceeds \texttt{max\_source\_height} (180 px). Removes LilyPond renders that wrapped onto two staves. \\
\texttt{filter\_non\_leadsheet\_clef} & True & Keeps only \texttt{clef:G2} (treble). Drops alto, tenor, bass and French-violin clefs inherited from orchestral PrIMuS sources. \\
\texttt{filter\_unusual\_time}       & True & Keeps only the eight common jazz meters: \{4/4, 3/4, 2/4, 2/2, 6/8, 6/4, 5/4, 12/8\}. \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\caption{Domain filters applied at training-set construction.}
\label{tab:filters}
\end{table}

The allowed time-signature set must remain in sync between \texttt{src/CRNN\_CTC/dataset.py} (\texttt{\_COMMON\_TIME\_SIGS}) and \texttt{src/omr\_pipeline/grammar\_fix.py} (\texttt{\_COMMON\_TIME\_SIGS}); the grammar fixer rejects, at inference, any prediction outside this set.
