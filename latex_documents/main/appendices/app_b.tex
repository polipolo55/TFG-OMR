\chapter{Qualitative Predictions and Training Diagnostics}
\label{app:qual}

This appendix collects the second through fifth worst-case predictions on the
synthetic scanned test split, selected by per-sample edit distance; the rank-1
case is shown in \Cref{sec:res-qualitative}.
Each figure pairs the scan-augmented input strip with a LilyPond
re-render of the grammar-fixed model prediction, so the discrepancy can
be read off the staves without consulting the raw token lists.
\Cref{tab:qual-worst-edits} summarises the edit-operation breakdown
for each sample.

\section{Worst-Case Predictions on the Synthetic Test Split}
\label{sec:qual-worst}

The rank-1 worst prediction is shown in \Cref{fig:qual-worst-1} of the
body (\Cref{sec:res-qualitative}).
\crefrange{fig:qual-worst-2}{fig:qual-worst-5} collect the next four
samples (ranks 2--5) with the highest edit distance on the scanned test
split (epoch-83 checkpoint, greedy decoding).
In every case the dominant failure mode is barline misplacement
(missed or spurious \code{measure} tokens), consistent with the
error budget of \Cref{sec:res-errors}; melodic content
(pitch, duration, accidental) is correct on most tokens even in
the worst predictions.

\section{Model Weight Distribution}
\label{app:weights}

The marginal distribution of the model's weights at convergence is a
useful diagnostic when comparing trained checkpoints.
\Cref{fig:weight-dist} shows the distributions of the four principal
parameter groups of the epoch-83 checkpoint: the classifier (final
linear layer), the convolutional and batch-normalisation weights of
the ResNet backbone, the LSTM weights, and the biases.

\begin{figure}[htbp]
    \centering
    \includegraphics[width=\textwidth]{figures/fig_weight_distributions}
    \caption{Marginal weight distributions of the four principal
    parameter groups of the trained CRNN at epoch 83.
    All four are close to centred Gaussian with standard deviations
    in the $[0.22, 0.35]$ range (the classifier layer is the widest);
    AdamW's \num{1e-4} weight decay keeps the magnitudes bounded, and
    no output row is dead.
    The small spikes near $\pm 1$ in the classifier panel are an
    artefact of the classifier saturating on the CTC blank class and
    the most frequent token.}
    \label{fig:weight-dist}
\end{figure}

All four groups sit within the magnitudes expected of a
well-regularised AdamW run; no parameter has grown to the
exploding-gradient regime that the \num{5.0} gradient-norm clip is
intended to prevent.
This distribution is reported as a sanity check on the training
procedure rather than as a primary result.
